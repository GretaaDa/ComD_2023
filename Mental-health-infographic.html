All you need to know about mental health based on US statistics

What is mental health?

Mental health is a state of well-being in which one's mind, emotions, thoughts and behaviors are in constant flux.

Mental health encompasses the complex texture of a person's psychological and emotional state, and is frequently referred to as the cornerstone of holistic well-being.

It creates the groundwork for a satisfying existence by reshaping emotions, ideas, actions, and moods.

Mental illnesses present in a variety of ways and always have distinct effects on the body. For example, eating disorders have a direct effect on the body, whereas depression has a significant impact on mental health.

More than 1 in 5 in the US have a mental illness

More than 1 in 5 of Americans suffer from mental illness; this percentage is also reflected in young people (13–18) who are dealing with significant mental health issues. Moreover, 1 in 25 Americans suffer from severe mental diseases.

The toolkit for mental health include counseling, assistance, and education. Customized treatment programs that incorporate therapy and support groups are essential for managing the distinct mental health path.

Responsive services connect people to essential resources in times of crisis. Comprehending medicine and investigating alternative remedies are given priority in treatment alternatives. Keep in mind this thought : You are not alone and will never be.

One persistent issue is that people are reluctant to talk about mental health, which leads to abuse and emphasizes how urgently society has to change. Encouraging candid dialogues becomes essential to normalizing conversations and providing assistance to individuals managing personal challenges.

In 2021, 22.8% of adult Americans suffered from a mental illness; the most common disorders were anxiety, depression, PTSD, bipolar disorder, and borderline personality disorder (BPD). Higher frequency is seen in the LGBT population (50.2%), among multiracial people (34.9%), and among American Indian communities (26.6%), according to demographic characteristics.

A variety of causes, including biological factors, childhood trauma, physical trauma, adult trauma, addiction, and social isolation, can contribute to mental health issues.

But there are some potential factors that can rase some issues for your mental wellbeing:
  • Childhood trauma – abuse, neglect, assault, violence
  • Chronic medical conditions – cancer, diabetes
  • Biological factors – chemical imbalance in the brain, genetics
  • Addiction – drugs, alcohol
  • Social isolation – lowliness, discrimination, poverty, bullying
  • Adult trauma – military, accident, violent crime
  • Physical trauma – head injury, epilepsy
