All You Need to Know About Mental Health Based on US Statistics

What is mental health?

Mental health is a state of well-being in which one's mind, emotions, thoughts and behaviors are in constant flux. It plays a huge part in everyday life. Everything from decision making to relationship building, and management of stress is affected by our mental wellbeing.

A yearly study done by Mental Health America shows that on average 21% of adults between the year 2019 & 2020, have experienced a mental illness in the US. That is equivalent to over 50 million of Americans.

The difference in percentages can be seen per state. Currently Utah is in the top with 29,68%.

Each year, around 1 in 5 of US citizens experience a mental disorder

The graph shows the anual percentages of the top 5 mental health disorders in the US.

The most common mental disorder that currently appear in 40 million Americans is - anxiety disorder.

Higher frequencies are seen in the LGBT population, multiracial people, and American Indian communities.

What are the risks?

15% of adults had substance use disorders in the past year

33.5% of adults in the US, that have a mental illness also experiences substance use

40% of people suffering from depression have a higher risk of developing cardiovascular & metabolic diseases

The rate of unemployment is higher in adults with mental illnesses than those without

Students with depression disorders less likely to graduate
